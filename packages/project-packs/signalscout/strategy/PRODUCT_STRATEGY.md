# Product Strategy: SignalScout

## Strategy Summary

SignalScout should win by staying focused on its wedge:

> A minimal revenue system for finding, qualifying, auditing, and contacting businesses with clear improvement opportunities.

## User Job

Users are not primarily buying features. They are trying to solve this job:

> Opportunity research and outreach are scattered across scraping, spreadsheets, manual audits, and generic messages that do not connect to a clear revenue workflow.

## MVP Bet

The first product bet is that users will value a focused, practical workflow more than a broad platform.

## What To Avoid

- Building generic features that weaken the wedge.
- Adding integrations before the core workflow is useful manually.
- Optimizing for impressive demos instead of repeated daily/weekly use.
- Confusing AI output with product value.

## Success Criteria

- A first-time user understands the product in under 60 seconds.
- The core workflow produces a useful result without manual explanation.
- The product creates a reason to return.
- The wedge remains visible in the UI, docs, and roadmap.
