# AI Council Logo Design Brief

## Purpose

Create the first production-minded logo draft for AI Council: a local-first AI Tech Lead and intelligent operating layer for builders. The logo must express expert coordination, governed intelligence, trustworthy decision-making, and modern AI infrastructure without becoming generic, mystical, or over-decorated.

This brief is written as a single handoff file for the designer or agent producing the first draft.

## Best Role To Take This Project

Act as a **Senior Brand Designer and Art Director for an AI developer platform**.

Use these lenses together:

- **Brand strategist:** protect positioning, differentiation, and memorability.
- **Logo designer:** simplify the mark until it works in one color and at small sizes.
- **Design systems lead:** enforce consistency across colors, geometry, spacing, sizes, and reusable variants.
- **Product UI designer:** make sure the identity can live inside a serious SaaS interface, not only on a presentation board.
- **Copywriter:** keep naming, tagline, and label language precise, mature, and ownable.

Do not act primarily as an illustrator, sci-fi concept artist, or decorative prompt artist. The goal is a durable identity system, not a one-off beautiful image.

## Project Context

AI Council is a local-first AI technical operating system for builders. It coordinates specialist agents, project memory, tool permissions, provider routing, evaluations, and governed execution.

The product should feel like:

- A senior technical partner.
- A council of expert agents.
- A controlled decision system.
- A practical builder tool.
- A trustworthy operating layer between human intent, AI models, tools, and project context.

The product should not feel like:

- A generic chatbot.
- A magical AI sparkle brand.
- A crypto/network token.
- A random agent swarm.
- A cold enterprise compliance product.
- A decorative neural-network poster.

## Brand DNA

The logo should be built from one brand idea:

```txt
Orchestrated Intelligence
```

This is the north star for the identity. The logo should not simply say "AI," "council," or "nodes." It should make intelligence feel coordinated, governed, and useful.

Brand pyramid:

```txt
Mission: Help builders make better technical decisions.
Promise: Every decision is evaluated.
Personality: Calm, precise, confident.
Visual language: Geometry, alignment, motion, proof.
Logo: Council Core.
```

Memorability goal:

- The mark should be remembered as a coordinated system moving toward one shared center.
- The silhouette should be distinctive before color, glow, or gradients are added.
- The central Council Core should become the most ownable part of the identity.

## Design Decision Memo

Recommended direction: **Structured Council Core**.

Use the cleaner system direction from the first provided identity board as the foundation: three intelligent nodes orbiting or aligning around a central Council Core. Borrow the more natural wordmark confidence from the second board: `AI Council` should usually read as title case, not widely tracked all-caps.

Confidence: medium-high.

Rationale:

- The three-node motif maps directly to council behavior, specialist agents, and collective reasoning.
- A central Council Core communicates shared context, governance, and decision synthesis.
- Title case feels more product-native and less cinematic than heavily spaced uppercase.
- A simplified geometric mark will scale better than a glowing, illustrative synapse icon.

Primary risk:

- Three connected nodes and AI sparkle shapes are common in AI branding. The central core must become more ownable and less generic.

Mitigation:

- Build a distinctive central glyph called the **Council Core**.
- Make the mark work without glow, gradients, or dark backgrounds.
- Define strict geometry, stroke, and spacing rules.

## Visual Concept

Create a symbol made from:

1. **Three outer nodes** representing specialist intelligences or senior agents.
2. **Three partial arcs or structured connections** representing coordination, routing, and shared workflow.
3. **One central Council Core** representing synthesis, judgment, context, and governed action.

The mark should read as a council, not merely a network.

Preferred metaphor:

- Coordinated experts around a shared Council Core.

Secondary metaphor:

- Intelligence routed through a governed operating layer.

Avoid making the mark look like:

- A generic molecule.
- A recycling symbol.
- A triquetra religious knot.
- A crypto token.
- A Bluetooth/network icon.
- A meditation symbol.
- A spaceship cockpit.
- A glowing neural explosion.

## Brand Physics

The mark should obey a small set of visual laws. These laws should eventually inform icons, loaders, product motion, and illustration.

The brand always moves toward:

- **Equilibrium:** separated parts align into a stable system.
- **Symmetry:** compositions are mathematically or optically balanced.
- **Minimum viable detail:** remove anything that does not improve recognition.
- **Governed motion:** movement feels deliberate, not energetic for its own sake.
- **Settled confidence:** the final state should feel resolved and calm.

Do not use motion, shape, or effects that imply chaos, magic, speed, explosion, or uncontrolled autonomy.

## Shape Grammar

Use a restricted shape vocabulary so the logo, icons, and product states feel related.

Allowed shapes:

- Circles.
- Rings.
- Partial arcs.
- Orbits.
- Intersections.
- Compass-like axes.
- Aperture-like cores.
- Clean circular cuts and negative space.

Avoid shapes:

- Hexagons.
- Lightning bolts.
- Explosions.
- Neural webs.
- Random polygons.
- Decorative stars.
- Organic tendrils.
- Complex particle fields.

The first draft should show discipline. If a shape does not support orchestration, alignment, or council behavior, remove it.

## Logo Structure

### Primary Mark

The primary logo mark should be a balanced circular-triangular system:

- Three outer nodes arranged at approximately 90 degrees, 210 degrees, and 330 degrees.
- One central Council Core placed exactly at the optical center.
- Outer arcs should imply connection without closing into a full circle.
- The top node should feel like a leader or anchor, but not dominate the other nodes.
- The lower two nodes should balance the composition symmetrically.

### Council Core

The central glyph is the most important distinctiveness opportunity.

Spend the majority of exploration time here. The Council Core may eventually become the favicon, loader, runtime icon, approval icon, evaluation icon, and memory symbol. It deserves more precision than a decorative sparkle.

Design requirements:

- Must work as a standalone micro-mark.
- Must be simpler than the current sparkle/star.
- Must not look like a generic four-point AI sparkle.
- Should suggest synthesis, evaluation, and decision.
- Should be built from the same geometry as the outer mark.

Possible directions:

- A four-point aperture with slightly concave sides.
- A three-axis compass spark.
- A small diamond-core with one vertical and one horizontal cut.
- A sharp but calm decision star derived from three intersecting paths.

Do not use a complex illustrated burst, lightning crack, or lens flare.

Future symbol family:

- **Orbital Core:** the full three-node logo system.
- **Council Core:** the central coordination glyph.
- **Agent Core:** a simplified node variant for specialist agents.
- **Runtime Core:** an active/process variant for execution states.
- **Approval Core:** a governed pause/authorization variant.

Do not design the full family in draft one, but make sure the Council Core can support it later.

### Wordmark

Use `AI Council` as the primary wordmark.

Recommended type treatment:

- Title case: `AI Council`.
- `AI` may carry a subtle gradient or color emphasis.
- `Council` should remain white or near-white in dark contexts.
- Use a clean geometric/humanist sans, with Inter acceptable for draft one.
- Avoid excessive letter spacing.
- Avoid spelling as `A I  C O U N C I L` for the main logo.

All-caps may be explored as a secondary lockup, but it should not be the primary direction unless it clearly outperforms title case.

## Color System

Use a tight, consistent palette. Do not introduce extra accent colors in the first draft.

### Core Palette

| Token | Hex | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Council Violet | `#785CFF` | Intelligence, top node, AI wordmark accent |
| Signal Blue | `#4DA3FF` | Analysis, left/lower node, primary interface accent |
| Synapse Cyan | `#00D4C8` | Collaboration, right/lower node, completion/action accent |
| Deep Space | `#0B0F17` | Primary dark background |
| Panel Navy | `#121826` | UI panels and app icon depth |
| Border Slate | `#1A2130` | Subtle rules, dividers, icon containers |
| Mist White | `#E6EAF2` | Primary text on dark |
| Pure White | `#FFFFFF` | High-emphasis text and white logo variant |

### Color Application Rules

- Use violet for the top node and AI emphasis.
- Use blue for the lower-left node.
- Use cyan for the lower-right node.
- Use the same node-color order in every logo version.
- Use arcs as a controlled gradient from violet to blue to cyan only when full color is allowed.
- Use solid colors in small sizes where gradients reduce clarity.
- Do not add pink, orange, green, or red to the core logo.
- Do not use more than three brand accents in the mark.
- Keep neutrals cool and dark; avoid beige, warm gray, brown, or heavy purple-only palettes.

### Gradient Rules

Gradient is allowed, but must be subtle and functional.

Allowed:

- Violet to blue.
- Blue to cyan.
- Violet to cyan only through a controlled midpoint.

Avoid:

- Heavy neon glow.
- Rainbow transitions.
- Large blurry halos.
- Gradients that make the logo illegible in mono.

## Shape And Geometry Rules

Consistency matters more than ornament.

### Grid

Build the mark on a square grid.

Recommended base:

- Artboard: `1024 x 1024`.
- Safe live area: `760 x 760`.
- Center point: `512, 512`.
- Outer node center radius: approximately `300 px` from center.
- Central core max size: `140 x 140 px`.

### Nodes

The three outer nodes must share identical shape logic.

Rules:

- Use perfect circles.
- Top node diameter: `108-128 px`.
- Lower node diameter: `108-128 px`.
- All three nodes should be equal size unless a tested optical correction is needed.
- If using inner highlights, keep them consistent across all nodes.
- Do not give each node a different 3D style.
- Do not let node highlights overpower the flat mark.

Optical correction:

- The nodes should be optically equal, not blindly mathematical.
- The top node may be adjusted by `2-3%` if needed to appear visually equal.
- Any optical correction must be documented in the geometry notes.
- Do not use optical correction as an excuse for inconsistent styling.

### Arcs

The arcs should imply a council loop without becoming a closed ring.

Rules:

- Use three separate arc segments.
- Arc stroke width: approximately `28-36 px` on a `1024 px` artboard.
- Arc endpoints should be rounded only if the nodes are also soft and modern.
- Keep arc gaps visually equal.
- Arc curvature should be circular and symmetrical.
- Do not mix thin arcs with thick nodes.
- Do not use organic tendril lines for the first draft.

### Central Core

Rules:

- Must align exactly to the center of the mark.
- Should be visually lighter than the nodes.
- Should have enough internal negative space to survive at small sizes.
- Use a simple filled or cutout shape.
- Avoid hairline strokes.
- Avoid tiny internal sparks.

Optical correction:

- Align the core mathematically first, then adjust optically only if the mark appears off-center.
- Preserve the feeling that all three nodes resolve into the same center.
- The core must remain readable after rasterization at `24 x 24 px`.

### Stroke Consistency

If strokes are used:

- Use one stroke family across iconography and logo.
- Stroke weight ratio should be consistent.
- Avoid combining thin line icons with heavy 3D logo nodes.
- At 24 px, strokes must still appear crisp.

### Corner And Radius Language

The logo uses circles and arcs. Product UI may use small rounded rectangles, but the mark itself should stay circular/geometric.

Rules:

- App icon corner radius should be controlled, not pill-like.
- Recommended app icon radius: `22-28%` of icon size.
- Avoid exaggerated soft-square backgrounds.
- Avoid placing the logo inside multiple nested rounded containers.

| Use | Target Size | Requirement |
|---|---:|---|
| Favicon | `16 x 16 px` | Must remain recognizable as a simple council/core mark. Remove arcs or use simplified mark if needed. |
| Small UI icon | `24 x 24 px` | Central core and three-node structure should still read. |

### Small-Size Rule

At `16 px`, use a simplified symbol:

- Three colored dots around a single central core, or
- Central Council Core only, if the full mark becomes muddy.

Do not force the full detailed mark into favicon size.

## Recognition And Trademark Readiness

The first draft must pass basic recognition stress tests before it is considered production-ready.

### Trademark Stress Test

Test the symbol in this sequence:

```txt
Remove gradients -> Remove glow -> Fill black -> Shrink -> Blur slightly -> Compare silhouette
```

The mark should still feel identifiable as AI Council. If it fails, simplify the silhouette or strengthen the Council Core.

### 300 Millisecond Test

Show the symbol for `300ms`, then hide it.

Ask:

- Did the viewer remember the three-part council structure?
- Did the viewer notice a central core?
- Could the viewer sketch the rough silhouette from memory?
- Did it feel like a serious AI product rather than a generic network mark?

If the answer is no, the logo is not distinctive enough yet.

- OpenAI.
- Anthropic.
- Claude.
- Cursor.
- Raycast.
- Linear.
- GitHub.
- Notion.
- Slack.

Audit for:

- Similar app icon framing.
- Similar motion metaphor.

The goal is not to avoid all category cues. The goal is to keep AI Council recognizably its own.

## Spacing And Lockup Rules

Use the symbol's node diameter as the spacing unit.

Let:

- `N` = outer node diameter.
- `S` = symbol width.

Primary horizontal lockup:

- Symbol height should match roughly `1.15x` the cap height of the wordmark.
- Gap between symbol and wordmark: `0.45N-0.65N`.
- Wordmark baseline should align optically to the vertical center of the symbol.
- Do not let the symbol feel like a decorative sticker beside the text.

Clear space:

- Minimum clear space around full logo: `1N`.
- Minimum clear space around symbol-only mark: `0.75N`.
- No text, border, or UI chrome should enter this area.

Wordmark spacing:

- Avoid wide tracking.
- Use natural title-case spacing.
- If all-caps is explored, tracking should be moderate and measured, not cinematic.

## Typography Direction

Use Inter for the first draft unless a clearly better typeface is justified.

Recommended:

- Wordmark: Inter SemiBold or custom-adjusted geometric sans.
- Product UI support: Inter.
- Tagline: Inter Regular or Medium.

Wordmark refinements:

- Consider slight custom cuts on `A`, `C`, or `I` only if they improve ownability.
- Do not over-customize the wordmark before the symbol is stable.
- Preserve readability over cleverness.

Tagline style:

- Smaller than wordmark.
- No wide tracking unless used sparingly.
- Should not compete with the symbol.

## Motion Direction

Motion is not required for the static first logo draft, but the mark should be designed so it can animate cleanly later.

Motion principle:

```txt
Separate -> Align -> Evaluate -> Settle
```

Motion should communicate:

- Alignment.
- Synchronization.
- Evaluation.
- Decision.
- Settling into confidence.

Motion should not communicate:

- Energy for its own sake.
- Excitement.
- Speed.
- Magic.
- Endless thinking.

Design implication:

- The three nodes should have clear paths toward the Council Core.
- The arcs should be able to reveal or align without changing shape language.
- The final resting state should be the most important frame.

## Acceptance Criteria

The first draft is successful if:

- The mark is recognizable without the wordmark.
- The mark works in one color.
- The mark works at `24 x 24 px`.
- A simplified version works at `16 x 16 px`.
- The three nodes are consistent in size, shape, and spacing.
- The arcs use consistent thickness, curvature, and gap logic.
- The central Council Core feels ownable and not like a generic AI sparkle.
- The color order is consistent: violet top, blue lower-left, cyan lower-right.
- The wordmark reads naturally as `AI Council`.
- The logo feels like a serious AI product for builders.
- The identity does not depend on glow, blur, or dark background effects.
- The silhouette is recognizable after the trademark stress test.
- The mark is memorable in a `300ms` recognition test.
- The Council Core can plausibly become a reusable product symbol.
- The shape grammar avoids hexagons, neural webs, lightning, explosions, and random polygons.

## Quality Gates

Before calling the draft complete, check:

- **Scalability:** test at `16 px`, `24 px`, `32 px`, `64 px`, and `256 px`.
- **Monochrome:** confirm the mark still communicates council/core without color.
- **Contrast:** verify dark and light background versions.
- **Geometry:** confirm nodes, arcs, and gaps are visually consistent.
- **Reproduction:** confirm it can be recreated as clean vector paths.
- **Differentiation:** compare mentally against generic AI network/spark logos and simplify or sharpen if it feels too familiar.
- **Product fit:** place it in a sidebar, app icon, and documentation header.
- **Competitive audit:** compare against OpenAI, Anthropic, Claude, Cursor, Raycast, Linear, GitHub, Notion, Slack, Figma, and Cisco.
- **Recognition:** run the black-fill, shrink, blur, and `300ms` tests before approving the silhouette.

## Risks And Watchouts

| Risk | Why It Matters | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Generic AI sparkle | Many AI brands use the same symbol language | Make the Council Core proprietary and geometry-derived |
| Over-glow | Looks good in a board but fails in product and print | Treat glow as optional presentation styling only |
| Too many gradients | Reduces consistency and small-size clarity | Limit gradient to the full-color hero logo |
| All-caps over-spacing | Can feel cinematic rather than operational | Use title-case wordmark as primary |
| Complex favicon | Full symbol may become muddy at 16 px | Create a dedicated simplified favicon |
| Inconsistent icon style | Logo, UI icons, and app icon may feel from different systems | Use shared stroke, radius, and color rules |
| Subconscious competitor similarity | AI, developer, and infrastructure marks share common geometry | Run a competitive silhouette audit before approval |
| Over-specified draft | Too many rules can slow exploration | Treat this brief as constraints for one focused vector direction, not a full identity constitution |
